# Deep Research Plan: Agent-Based Models for Consumer Goods Markets

**Slug:** abm-consumer-goods-markets
**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Status:** AWAITING APPROVAL

## Key Questions

1. **What ABM frameworks and architectures have been applied to consumer goods markets?** (e.g., grocery, FMCG, durable goods, fashion)
2. **How are consumer agents typically modeled?** (decision rules, bounded rationality, social influence, brand loyalty, habit formation)
3. **How are firm/producer agents modeled?** (pricing, inventory, product innovation, advertising, competitive strategy)
4. **What market mechanisms and interaction topologies are used?** (matching, posted prices, auctions, network effects, retail intermediaries)
5. **How are these models calibrated and validated?** (micro data, scanner data, stylized facts, empirical benchmarks)
6. **What are the key application domains?** (marketing strategy, supply chain, policy analysis, competition/antitrust, sustainability)
7. **What are the main platforms/toolkits used?** (Mesa, NetLogo, Repast, MASON, custom)
8. **What are the open challenges and frontier directions?** (LLM-powered agents, digital twins, real-time calibration, scalability)

## Evidence Needed

- Academic literature on ABMs in consumer/retail/FMCG markets (seminal + recent)
- Survey papers or book chapters on ABM marketing/consumer behavior
- Specific model architectures (e.g., Tesfatsion, Hamill & Gilbert, Rand & Rust)
- Empirical calibration case studies (scanner data, panel data)
- Industry/commercial applications (if any — consulting, CPG firms)
- Software toolkits and reproducibility status
- Recent advances: LLM agents, generative agents in market simulation
