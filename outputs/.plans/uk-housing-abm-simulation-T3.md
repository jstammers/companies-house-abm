# T3: Mortgage & Default Modelling in ABMs, Stress Testing, Macro Shock Transmission

## Objective
Investigate how agent-based models represent mortgage origination, pricing, default, and how external macro shocks transmit into housing outcomes.

## Topics to Cover

### Mortgage Modelling in ABMs
- How do ABMs model mortgage origination decisions (affordability checks, LTV/LTI limits)?
- How are mortgage interest rates set (spread over base rate, risk-based pricing)?
- Fixed vs variable rate representation
- Mortgage product switching / refinancing behaviour
- Buy-to-let mortgage modelling

### Default & Arrears Modelling
- What triggers default in housing ABMs (negative equity + income shock, debt-service-ratio thresholds)?
- How is forbearance modelled?
- Forced sale / repossession dynamics and their feedback to prices
- Comparison with traditional PD/LGD models used in banking

### Macro Shock Transmission Channels
- **Interest rate changes** → mortgage payments → affordability → demand → prices
- **Unemployment shocks** → income loss → default + reduced demand
- **Immigration / population growth** → demand pressure
- **Planning policy / housing supply** → supply constraints
- **Government interventions** — Help to Buy, stamp duty holidays, Right to Buy
- **Inflation** → real income erosion, construction costs
- **Credit conditions** → LTV/LTI tightening/loosening

### Stress Testing
- How do central banks (BoE, ECB) use or consider ABMs for stress testing?
- Comparison of ABM stress testing vs. traditional econometric approaches
- Advantages: heterogeneity, nonlinearity, emergent fire-sale dynamics

## Questions to Answer
1. Which ABMs have the most sophisticated mortgage/default modules?
2. How do ABMs capture the feedback loop between defaults, forced sales, and falling prices?
3. What external factors have been modelled and which are missing?
4. How do ABM results compare with traditional stress test outputs?
