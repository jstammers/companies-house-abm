# T1: UK Housing ABM Academic Literature

## Objective
Survey the academic literature on agent-based models applied to the UK housing market. Identify key models, their architectures, findings, and limitations.

## Key Models to Investigate
- **Baptista et al. (2016)** — BoE Staff Working Paper 619: "Macroprudential policy in an agent-based model of the UK housing market"
- **Carro et al. (2023)** — "Heterogeneous effects of monetary policy on housing market activity" or related BoE ABM work
- **Ge (2017)** — MASON-based UK housing ABM
- **Geanakoplos et al. (2012)** — Leverage cycle ABM (US-focused but influential)
- **Pangallo et al.** — Housing market ABM work
- **Axtell et al.** — Large-scale housing ABM (Washington DC, but architecturally relevant)
- Any ECB, Bundesbank, or other central bank housing ABMs

## Questions to Answer
1. What agent types are used (buyers, sellers, renters, BTL investors, banks, developers)?
2. How is the housing market clearing mechanism modelled (auction, posted price, bilateral matching)?
3. What behavioural rules govern agents (adaptive expectations, reference-dependent preferences, heuristics)?
4. What stylised facts do these models reproduce (fat tails, volatility clustering, boom-bust, spatial patterns)?
5. What are the main limitations identified by the authors?
6. How do models handle spatial heterogeneity (regional, local authority, postcode level)?

## Output
Write findings to `outputs/.drafts/uk-housing-abm-simulation-research-T1.md` with full source citations (paper title, authors, year, arXiv ID or DOI/URL where available). Use web search and paper search tools. Do NOT fetch PDF files directly — use abstracts, metadata, and web pages.
