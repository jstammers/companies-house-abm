# T3: Firm Agents, Market Mechanisms, and Calibration

## Objective
Research how firm/producer agents are modeled, what market mechanisms are used, and how ABMs for consumer goods are calibrated and validated against real data.

## Tasks
1. Search for firm agent models in consumer goods ABMs — pricing, inventory, advertising, product innovation
2. Find market mechanism designs — posted prices, auctions, matching, retail intermediaries, platforms
3. Look for supply chain ABMs in consumer goods (manufacturer-distributor-retailer)
4. Identify calibration approaches — scanner data, panel data, GfK/Nielsen, synthetic data
5. Find validation methodologies — stylized facts reproduction, out-of-sample prediction, sensitivity analysis

## What to Record
For each important source:
- Title, authors, year, venue, identifier (DOI/arXiv ID/URL)
- Firm decision model type
- Market mechanism
- Calibration data source and method
- Validation approach

## Output
Write findings to `outputs/.drafts/abm-consumer-goods-markets-research-T3.md`

## Constraints
- Use paper search (alpha tools) and web search
- Do NOT fetch PDFs or call alpha_get_paper — use abstracts, metadata, and web pages only
- Minimum 5 distinct search queries
