# T2: Consumer Agent Decision Models and Social Influence

## Objective
Research how consumer agents are modeled in ABMs for consumer goods markets — their decision rules, cognitive architectures, social influence mechanisms, and behavioral realism.

## Tasks
1. Search for how consumer choice is modeled in ABMs (utility maximization, heuristics, bounded rationality, satisficing)
2. Find models incorporating social influence / word-of-mouth / network effects on purchasing
3. Look for brand loyalty, habit formation, and switching costs in ABM consumers
4. Identify how heterogeneity is introduced (income, preferences, demographics, psychographics)
5. Find comparisons between ABM consumer models and traditional discrete choice / conjoint approaches

## What to Record
For each important source:
- Title, authors, year, venue, identifier (DOI/arXiv ID/URL)
- Decision rule type (utility, heuristic, BDI, reinforcement learning, etc.)
- Social influence mechanism if any
- Whether empirically calibrated

## Output
Write findings to `outputs/.drafts/abm-consumer-goods-markets-research-T2.md`

## Constraints
- Use paper search (alpha tools) and web search
- Do NOT fetch PDFs or call alpha_get_paper — use abstracts, metadata, and web pages only
- Minimum 5 distinct search queries
