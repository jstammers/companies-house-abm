# T2: UK Housing Data Landscape, Commercial Tools & Regulatory Context

## Objective
Map the UK housing data ecosystem, existing commercial scenario-planning tools, and the regulatory context that creates demand for housing/mortgage stress testing.

## Data Sources to Investigate
- **Land Registry Price Paid Data** — transaction-level house prices, coverage, update frequency
- **ONS House Price Index (HPI)** — methodology (hedonic vs repeat-sales vs mix-adjusted), regional breakdowns
- **FCA Product Sales Data (PSD001/PSD007)** — mortgage origination microdata, what fields are available
- **English Housing Survey** — household characteristics, tenure, housing conditions
- **BoE mortgage lending statistics** — aggregate flows, rates, LTV distributions
- **HMRC Stamp Duty Land Tax data** — transaction volumes by price band
- **Zoopla/Rightmove listing data** — asking prices, time on market (commercial data)

## Commercial Tools to Investigate
- **Moody's Analytics** — HPA (House Price Appreciation) models, ResiLandscape
- **Oxford Economics** — UK Housing Model
- **CoreLogic** — house price analytics
- **Nationwide/Halifax HPI** — methodology and use
- **Any ABM-based commercial products** in housing/mortgage space

## Regulatory Context
- **PRA stress testing** — how banks stress mortgage portfolios, what scenarios are prescribed
- **FPC macroprudential tools** — LTV/LTI flow limits, countercyclical buffer
- **FCA affordability assessment** — mortgage affordability rules post-MMR
- **Basel IRB** — mortgage risk weight models

## Questions to Answer
1. What data is freely available vs. requires licensing for a commercial product?
2. What do existing commercial tools offer and what are their limitations?
3. What gap would an ABM-based tool fill that current econometric models don't?
4. What regulatory requirements create demand for scenario planning tools?
